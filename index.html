SLIME MOLD SPREAD SIMULATION

This is a simulation of the spread of Physarum polycephalum, a slime mold A.K.A. the blob. It's an organism that is not an animal, nor a plant, nor a fungus. Its known for its ability to create efficient and intelligent networks and solving "shortest path" problems.

How to use the simulation

  • Click anywhere on the black square to create a root blob.
  • Hold Shift and click anywhere to delete cells.
  • Hold Ctrl and click anywhere to create a permanent root blob

Math & Physics at work:
This simulation is based on the cellular automata model. THe plane is composed of two layers: the food trail layer and the position layer.

Every slime node has three sensors. A sensor detects the amount of food in its sensor's area. The slime moves forward according to its sensors' values. These are detailed in the figures below. A slime node starts by "sensing" (evaluating the values of its sensors) If the middle sensor detects the most food out of the 3 sensors, it doesn't change direction. If both side sensors detect more food than the middle sensor, it turns randomly. If the right sensor detects more food than the middle sensor, it turns right. If the left sensor detects more food than the middle sensor, it turns left. Then it moves forward according to that direction. Then it deposits food on the food trail layer.
